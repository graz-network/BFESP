# Mandatory citation style policy

This policy is mandatory for all full article drafts and final LaTeX packaging.

## Core rule

Choose one citation style and apply it consistently across:
- in-text citations
- bibliography formatting
- archive-source labeling
- notes about missing metadata

Do not mix styles inside the same article.

## Default policy

Unless the user specifies a target journal with another requirement, use:
- citation style: APA 7 compatible author-date logic
- bibliography implementation: `biblatex` with author-date output
- archive sources: cite as primary or organizational materials with stable titles and dates

## Mandatory distinctions

Distinguish clearly between:
1. scholarly secondary literature
2. primary organizational or archive documents
3. the anchor report itself

Do not allow archive materials to masquerade as peer-reviewed literature.
Do not flatten all sources into one undifferentiated reference practice.

## In-text citation rules

Apply these rules:
- cite interpretive, comparative, and theoretical claims
- cite archival claims when they depend on specific years, documents, or organizational decisions
- cite the anchor report whenever describing its original arguments, methods, or proposals
- avoid citation padding where multiple references add no argumentative value

## Metadata discipline

When metadata is incomplete:
- do not invent authors, dates, or publishers
- keep the entry partial
- prefer corporate authors where appropriate
- preserve stable titles or filenames when needed for archive traceability
- mention uncertainty in a note only when it matters to interpretation

## Direct quotation rules

Use direct quotations sparingly.
When quoting:
- preserve the source language unless translation is required for the article language
- indicate translation responsibility if you translate meaningfully
- prefer paraphrase when the claim matters more than the phrasing

## Bilingual rule

The English and French articles may have language-appropriate bibliography formatting details,
but both must obey the same underlying citation logic and source distinctions.

## Final compliance check

Before packaging, verify all of the following:
- every in-text citation has a bibliography entry
- every bibliography entry is cited or intentionally retained
- citation style is internally consistent
- archive and scholarly sources are not conflated
- anchor report citations are present where needed
