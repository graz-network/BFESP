# Output specification

Use this output contract unless the user asks for a narrower subset.

## Standard staged deliverables

1. source map
2. anchor PDF review bundle
3. BFESP archive memorandum
4. Talent Romandie yearly summaries
5. longitudinal Talent Romandie synthesis
6. five expert memoranda
7. style decision note
8. extension plan
9. article blueprint with word budgets
10. bibliography audit
11. final article draft in English
12. final article draft in French
13. final preflight note
14. LaTeX package
15. PDF package when tool support is available

## Final package structure

```text
project-output/
  english_article.tex
  french_article.tex
  english_article.pdf
  french_article.pdf
  bibliography.bib
  figures/
  tables/
  preflight_note.md
```

If only one language is requested, omit the other language outputs.

## Minimum article qualities

Each final article should aim for:
- about 20 pages in standard academic formatting
- about 7,000 to 8,500 words in the main text
- a clear research question
- a defensible originality claim
- archive-grounded synthesis
- explicit discussion of limitations
- clear conclusion and next research step
- one explicit target style profile
- one consistent citation style

## Default section budgets

Use the stricter outline in `references/article-outline.md`.
Default targets:
- abstract: 180 to 250 words
- introduction: 700 to 900 words
- background and original report context: 700 to 900 words
- analytical framework and corpus: 750 to 950 words
- global synthesis of Rapport-FEP-Graz: 1,100 to 1,400 words
- historical extension from BFESP and Talent Romandie archives: 1,000 to 1,250 words
- coaching systems and technical development analysis: 1,000 to 1,200 words
- performance sport psychology analysis: 800 to 1,050 words
- discussion and originality claim: 900 to 1,100 words
- conclusion: 350 to 500 words

## Evidence discipline

When archive coverage is partial:
- state that coverage is partial
- separate evidence from inference
- avoid pretending that missing years or missing documents are complete

## Bibliography discipline

Use the workflow in `references/bibliography-workflow.md`.
The final bibliography must distinguish:
- anchor report references
- archive primary sources
- new scholarly framing references

## Language discipline

All skill instructions, notes, and structural guidance stay in English.
Only the French researcher output may be written in French.
