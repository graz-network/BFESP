# Final article outline and word budgets

Use this outline by default when the user asks for the full article package.
Deviate only when the archive clearly supports a better structure and explain why.

## Target length

- Main text target: 7,000 to 8,500 words
- Abstract: 180 to 250 words
- Excludes references, appendices, figure captions, and tables
- This length is intended to yield about 20 pages in standard academic formatting

## Default section plan

1. **Title**
   - concise and analytical
   - make the archive extension visible in the title or subtitle

2. **Abstract** — 180 to 250 words
   Cover:
   - topic and context
   - source base
   - research question
   - method of archive-based synthesis
   - core findings
   - contribution

3. **Keywords** — 5 to 7 terms
   Use a mix of domain and method labels.

4. **Introduction** — 700 to 900 words
   Must do all of the following:
   - introduce the problem
   - locate the original report historically and institutionally
   - state the research question
   - state the article thesis
   - preview the archive extension strategy
   - preview the article structure

5. **Background and original report context** — 700 to 900 words
   Cover:
   - original coaching setting
   - authorial objective of the report
   - Talent Romandie and Swiss cycling context
   - status of the original document as coaching/report literature rather than a journal article

6. **Analytical framework and corpus** — 750 to 950 words
   Cover:
   - source classes
   - role of `pdf-review-workflow` in the initial reading
   - logic of BFESP archive analysis
   - logic of yearly Talent Romandie synthesis
   - analytical role of the five expert lenses
   - evidence limits and missing materials

7. **Global synthesis of Rapport-FEP-Graz** — 1,100 to 1,400 words
   This section must summarize the report as a whole, not only one chapter.
   Cover:
   - discipline and athlete analysis
   - annual planning and load logic
   - training and competition reasoning
   - technical/freestyle contribution
   - strengths, limitations, and unrealized implications

8. **Historical extension from BFESP and Talent Romandie archives** — 1,000 to 1,250 words
   Cover:
   - year-by-year developments condensed into a narrative
   - continuity versus change across the archive
   - institutional, pedagogical, and performance evolution
   - what later materials confirm, complicate, or supersede

9. **Coaching systems and technical development analysis** — 1,000 to 1,200 words
   Combine the cycling coaching, sports coaching, freestyle sport expert, and olympic cross-country mountain biking expert lenses.
   Cover:
   - periodization and athlete development logic
   - technical skill development
   - coaching ecology
   - family, club, and regional structure
   - what can be generalized

10. **Performance sport psychology analysis** — 800 to 1,050 words
    Cover:
    - motivation
    - confidence
    - concentration
    - self-regulation and fatigue perception
    - autonomy and developmental transitions
    - missing or underdeveloped psychological mechanisms in the original report

11. **Discussion and originality claim** — 900 to 1,100 words
    This is the article's argumentative core.
    Cover:
    - what is academically original here
    - how the archive changes the reading of the original report
    - what the article contributes to youth cycling coaching knowledge
    - methodological limits of archive-based reconstruction

12. **Conclusion** — 350 to 500 words
    Cover:
    - answer to the research question
    - concise takeaways
    - practical implications
    - next research step

## Word-budget discipline

- Stay within about plus or minus 10 percent of each section budget
- If a section must expand, reduce another section to keep the whole article near target length
- Do not let historical description crowd out analysis
- Do not let the sport psychology section become a token add-on
- Do not let the discussion become a repetition of earlier sections

## Section-level evidence rule

Each main section after the introduction should explicitly indicate one or more of:
- anchor report evidence
- BFESP archive evidence
- Talent Romandie longitudinal evidence
- expert-lens interpretation

## Figures and tables

Use only when they sharpen the argument.
Default candidates:
- corpus table
- year-by-year archive table
- analytical map of the original report
- comparative table of preserved versus revised concepts
