# Expert lenses

Use these lenses after the archive has been mapped and the anchor PDF has been reviewed.

## Cycling coaching expert

Primary question:
How strong is the project as a cycling-performance and technical-development contribution?

Evaluate:
- periodization and annual planning
- technical progression
- competition preparation
- realism of workload and control methods
- integration of multidisciplinary bike skills
- transfer to XCO youth development

Look for:
- coaching insights worth preserving as-is
- points that need empirical strengthening
- points that became stronger or weaker in later archive materials

## Sports coaching expert

Primary question:
How strong is the project as a coaching-system and athlete-development model?

Evaluate:
- organizational design
- relation between club, family, coach, and regional structure
- talent pathway logic
- pedagogical sequencing
- athlete autonomy and responsibility
- monitoring and decision-making structures

Look for:
- generalizable coaching principles
- assumptions that only work in a narrow context
- elements that can support a stronger academic discussion section

## Performance sport psychology expert

Primary question:
What are the psychological mechanisms already present, missing, or only implicit in the project?

Evaluate:
- motivation
- confidence
- concentration
- emotional regulation
- fatigue perception and monitoring
- transition and adolescence issues
- family coaching dynamics
- autonomy, competence, and relatedness

Look for:
- explicit psychology already inside the report
- concepts that can be formalized more rigorously
- missing psychological variables the archive now makes discussable

## Freestyle sport expert

Primary question:
How strong is the report's freestyle contribution as a model for technical innovation, skill transfer, and creative motor learning?

Evaluate:
- freestyle, BMX, pumptrack, DH, and trail influences on XCO technique development
- technical transfer claims from freestyle disciplines into cross-country performance settings
- use of play, experimentation, free riding, and non-linear learning
- role of body-bike dissociation, proactive riding, and movement variability
- pedagogical value of flat pedals, lowered saddle work, and movement decomposition
- plausibility of a multidisciplinary technical model across cycling disciplines

Look for:
- the strongest freestyle-derived concepts that deserve preservation
- claims that need stronger evidence or narrower wording
- distinctions between practical coaching innovation and academic generalization
- ways to integrate the freestyle contribution without overstating causal proof

## Olympic cross-country mountain biking (XCO) expert

Primary question:
How strong is the project specifically as an XCO performance, training, and race-demands contribution rather than as a generic cycling or technical coaching text?

Evaluate:
- specificity of XCO physiological demands, including repeated supra-threshold surges, climbing power demands, and variable pacing
- fit between technical-development claims and modern XCO course demands
- race-format logic, lap-based demands, and positioning constraints at youth and elite levels
- interaction between technical skill, pacing, tactical positioning, and fatigue resistance
- adequacy of training load, periodization, and competition preparation for XCO development pathways
- transferability of the report's recommendations to contemporary XCO coaching practice

Look for:
- XCO-specific insights that should be highlighted rather than diluted inside broader cycling analysis
- claims that are discipline-specific to XCO and should not be generalized to all cycling contexts
- where the archive helps update the 2016 report for modern XCO practice
- concrete ways to sharpen the article's contribution to Olympic cross-country mountain biking scholarship and coaching
