# Journal and article style selector

Select one style profile before drafting the final paper.
Do not leave the article in an undefined generic mode.
Record the chosen profile explicitly in the blueprint.

## Selection rule

Choose in this order:
1. user-specified journal if provided
2. user-specified article type if no journal is provided
3. the closest profile below
4. if nothing is specified, default to `generic sport-science`

## Required selector output

Produce a short style decision note with:
- selected profile
- intended readership
- article type
- sectioning pattern
- tone and density expectations
- citation expectations
- figure and table expectations
- abstract and title conventions
- any constraints imposed on the English and French researchers

## Default profiles

### 1. Generic sport-science
Use when no journal is specified.

Characteristics:
- audience: applied sport science and coaching research readers
- article type: archive-based analytical paper or conceptual case extension
- tone: formal, analytic, readable
- method visibility: explicit but not over-technical
- citation density: moderate to strong where claims are interpretive
- tables: welcomed when they clarify chronology, corpus, or analytical contrasts
- title pattern: analytic title with a clarifying subtitle

### 2. Applied coaching and pedagogy
Use when the user wants stronger practical relevance.

Characteristics:
- audience: coaches, coach educators, talent developers
- article type: practice-based research or applied conceptual paper
- tone: direct, practice-linked, still academic
- method visibility: enough to justify evidence use, not heavy theory signaling
- citation density: moderate, concentrated in framing and discussion
- tables: helpful for models, coaching phases, progression frameworks
- title pattern: coaching problem plus applied contribution

### 3. Sport psychology oriented
Use when the user wants psychological interpretation foregrounded.

Characteristics:
- audience: sport psychology and performance psychology readers
- article type: conceptual-interpretive archive paper with applied discussion
- tone: conceptually precise, more theory-linked
- method visibility: explicit on inference boundaries
- citation density: stronger around psychological constructs and discussion
- tables: use sparingly unless they clarify constructs or archive periods
- title pattern: psychological theme plus archive or coaching context

### 4. Interdisciplinary technique and skill acquisition
Use when the user wants the freestyle and technical innovation dimension foregrounded.

Characteristics:
- audience: skill acquisition, coaching science, technique development readers
- article type: interdisciplinary conceptual paper anchored in archive material
- tone: analytically bold but evidence-disciplined
- method visibility: clear on where conceptual extension begins
- citation density: strong in framing the transfer and motor-learning discussion
- tables: use for movement models, progression logic, and cross-discipline mapping
- title pattern: technical development problem plus interdisciplinary contribution

## Bilingual rule

The English and French researcher agents must follow the same style profile.
They may adapt prose rhythm to language norms, but not the structural or citation logic.
