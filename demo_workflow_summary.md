- analyzes the full 2016 report,
- identifies what is still strong as coaching knowledge,

## 2. Anchor PDF review bundle
### Summary
The 2016 report is a multi-part coaching document combining discipline analysis, athlete analysis, annual planning, a technical training intervention, a competition analysis, and a freestyle-informed technical chapter.

### Academic review
**Main contribution:** a coherent practical architecture for youth XCO coaching that links load management, technical development, competition reasoning, and multidisciplinary bike-skill transfer.

**Method / reasoning style:** applied training-science reasoning, case illustration, observation, and program design.

**Strengths:**
- unusually coherent internal structure,
- detailed annual planning and load-monitoring logic,
- serious treatment of technical development,
- early attempt to theorize freestyle-derived technical learning.

**Weaknesses:**
- no formal empirical method,
- evidence mostly internal and observational,
- single-case illustration,
- psychological constructs present but not explicitly theorized.

### Main arguments
1. Youth XCO needs an integrated approach linking endurance, technique, and pacing.
2. Regional development structures should complement clubs and families rather than replace them.
3. Freestyle/BMX/DH/pumptrack can enrich technical development if used as pedagogical tools.

### Next steps translated into extension plan
- Test whether the technical-first logic survives in later Talent Romandie planning documents.
- Show how evaluation and structured training were progressively formalized.
- Reframe implicit psychological themes using more explicit language.
- Narrow the originality claim from causal proof to archive-based reconstruction.

## 3. BFESP archive memorandum
### 2018
- Diagnoses technical level as a reason for weak Romand representation in national top-30 standings.
- Proposes more weekend sessions, more cross-disciplinary technical work, and evaluation-linked Talent Card distribution.

### 2019-20
- Reports growth from 4 to 17 training opportunities.
- Keeps technique central while adding physiological training linked to technical circuits.
- Formalizes camp and selection criteria around presence, evaluation, results, form, and coach judgment.

### 2020-21
- Formalizes committee and coaching roles.
- Recasts project identity around the triangle: technical priority -> athlete evaluation -> structured training.
- Quantifies the project with athlete count, coach count, training hours, and competitive targets.

## 4. Talent Romandie chronology pass
### 2018
Key theme: strategic correction. Technical weakness is diagnosed and cross-disciplinary technical sessions are proposed.

### 2019-20
Key theme: consolidation. Technical work remains central while physiological work is added in a controlled way.

### 2020-21
Key theme: institutionalization. The model is simplified into a stable organizational language, backed by values, coach roles, and quantified targets.

### Longitudinal synthesis
**Stable:** technical priority, regional complementarity, multidisciplinary practice.

**Evolving:** formal evaluation, camp criteria, coach governance, parent-facing communication.

**Intensifying:** coupling of technical and physiological training.

**Potentially dated:** strong reliance on internal evaluation metrics and motor-test-centered language as selection tools.

## 5. Expert memoranda (condensed)
### Cycling coaching expert
Preserve the load-management logic, pacing analysis, and technical progression. Strengthen efficacy claims by narrowing them.

### Sports coaching expert
The most generalizable principle is the ecological design: the regional structure complements clubs and families instead of displacing them.

### Performance sport psychology expert
The archive makes visible autonomy support, competence/confidence building, concentration, and fatigue self-monitoring, but these remain under-formalized.

### Freestyle sport expert
The strongest practical contribution is not direct causal transfer from BMX/DH to XCO, but richer technical learning environments built around movement variability and proactive riding.

### XCO expert
The documents align well with modern XCO demands: variable pacing, repeated surges, technical control under fatigue, and position-dependent racing.
